## Project 1: Hydrophilicity-Based Epitope Prediction
(epitopeprediction.ipynb)

The identification of antigenic regions within protein is an important aspect in vaccine design and immunological research. These antigenic areas known as epitopes can be predicted on the basis of hydrophilicity. Hydrophilic amino acid residues are more likely to be exposed on the surface of proteins and are therefore accessible to antibody recognition. In this project, hydrophilicity analysis was used to identify potential B-cell epitopes within a selected protein sequence obtained from the NCBI Protein Database. 
- Retrieved protein sequences of SARS-CoV-2 Spike Glycoprotein (S protein) from NCBI using Biopython
- Performed hydrophilicity analysis
- Predicted potential B-cell epitopes
- Visualized results using Matplotlib

## Project 2: Comparative Analysis of Insulin Protein Sequences Across Species       
(evolutionaryanalysis.ipynb)

Evolutionary biology seeks to understand how organisms and their biological molecules have changed over time. Proteins that perform similar functions in different species often share common evolutionary origins and may exhibit conserved sequence regions. In this project, insulin protein sequences from multiple mammalian species, including human, mouse, rat, cow, and pig, were analysed using computational methods. By comparing these sequences, evolutionary similarities and differences were identified, providing insights into protein conservation across species.
-Retrieved insulin protein sequences of Human,Mouse,Rat,Cow,Pig from NCBI and compare them to study evolutionary conservation
- Used Biopython and Pandas for sequence analysis
- Investigated evolutionary conservation patterns
